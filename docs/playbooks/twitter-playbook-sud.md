## POSTING RULES

### Format mix (weekly targets)
| Format | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Standalone post | 5-6/week | Primary format. Under 280 chars. |
| Thread | 1/week | Only when one post genuinely can't hold the idea |
| Quote-RT with take | 2-3/week | Add a specific POV, don't just amplify |
| Reply | 5-10/day | Engagement. Patterns 1-4 from voice playbook. |

### Hook rules (Harry Dry's 3 tests — every post must pass all 3)
1. **Can I visualise it?** — abstract claims fail. "AI will change everything" fails. "GPT-4o outperformed radiologists on chest X-ray classification" passes.
2. **Can I falsify it?** — if nobody could disagree, it's not a hook. It's a caption.
3. **Can nobody else say this?** — if any account in your niche could post this, rewrite until only you can.

### Hook formulas for Sud's pillars

**AI Innovations:**
- `[Model/tool] just [specific thing it can do]. [one implication nobody's saying].`
- `the benchmark that matters for [use case] isn't [common one]. it's [actual one].`
- `[AI company] shipping [feature] is [counterintuitive interpretation].`

**Sports & Cricket:**
- `[Player]'s [specific decision in match context] was [bold take]. here's why.`
- `[BCCI/selectors] did [thing]. [one-line snark].`
- `unpopular: [specific falsifiable cricket opinion].`

**eSports & Dota 2:**
- `[patch number] broke [mechanic]. [who benefits and who doesn't].`
- `[team]'s draft in game [N] was [take]. [specific reason].`
- `[hero] is [state] right now because [specific mechanical reason].`

**Literature:**
- `[Book title] is [one-word adjective]. here's the part that stuck.`
- `abandoned [book] 40 pages in. [specific reason]. no regrets.`
- `[Author]'s [book] and [unrelated thing] are the same argument.`

**Gaming & Experimental Cooking:**
- `experiment: [hypothesis]. result: [honest outcome, usually bad].`
- `[game title] does [specific mechanic] better than anything I've played. [why].`
- `[cooking technique] applied to [ingredient]. update: [result].`

## ENGAGEMENT RULES

### Daily targets
| Action | Target |
|---|---|
| Replies (quality) | 5-10 |
| Quote-RTs with take | 2-3 |
| Likes | 20+ on relevant content |

### Reply rules
- Never reply with just agreement. Minimum: agreement + one specific add.
- Avoid replying to accounts with 100K+ followers unless you have a genuinely specific take.
- Reply early on threads gaining traction in your pillars. First 30 minutes of a thread matter most for visibility.
- No "great thread!" replies. No "this 👆" replies.

### Search terms to monitor daily
- `AI agents`, `LLM inference`, `model release` — for AI Innovations
- `IPL 2026`, `India cricket`, `Test series` — for Sports & Cricket
- `Dota 2 patch`, `TI qualifier`, `SEA Dota` — for eSports & Dota 2
- `book recommendation`, `what to read` — for Literature

## CTA STRATEGY

### While newsletter_url is empty (current state)
- No hard CTAs. No "follow me for more."
- Soft close: end threads with a question that invites reply, not a plug.
- Bio does the work: "AI nerd. Cricket, Dota 2, books, experimental cooking. All personal."

### When newsletter launches (set newsletter_url in config.json)
- BOFU posts activate automatically. Add "subscribe at [url]" as thread closer.
- Update bio to include newsletter link.

## Trend Update — 2026-03-23

1. Emphasize Personal Storytelling: Posts that integrate personal stories, experiences, or opinions about a game, book, or cooking experiment resonate more. Use phrases like “here’s my experience with...” to draw readers in.

2. Utilize Visual Content: Incorporate vibrant images or short clips related to your posts, especially in standalone posts. For instance, an image of a dish from an experimental cook or a highlight reel from a recent cricket match can enhance engagement.

3. Adopt Current Conversations: Engage directly with trending topics within your niches. For example, comment on recent match outcomes or new game patches by using direct questions or challenges that invite responses, such as “Did you see how [Player X] performed? Here's my take.”

4. Stronger Calls to Action: Even without a newsletter URL, prompt engagement by asking questions or encouraging retweets of controversial opinions. Use CTAs like “What do you think about this?” or “Who else agrees?”

5. Integrate Humor and Snark: Incorporate light-hearted commentary or snarky takes to build a distinctive voice. For example, instead of stating facts, frame them humorously, such as, “The selectors must have had a rough week if they picked that lineup.” This can help create shareable content.
